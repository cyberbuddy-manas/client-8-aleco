1. Casement UPVC Windows

Casement windows are the most popular type of frame frames. They are also known as crank windows. These windows have hinges on the side walls structuree frame that allow them to swing inwards and outwards. Depending on the style of the frame, casement windows may complement classic or modern décor.

This UPVC window design is lockable and thus built for safety. However, they are not the ideal choice if you're looking at installing window air conditioning units. You may also be in trouble if you leave the windows open on a rainy day.

2. Fixed UPVC Window Design

Fixed windows or picture windows are self-explanatory. These windows are permanently fixed and do not open or close. Since they are fixed, they may be any size of shape and require minimal hardware.

Their function is mainly ornamental and to bring in natural light into the room while keeping dust, pollution and noise outside. Fixed UPVC windows with grills are often installed in staircases, bathrooms, attics and hallways. They are also installed in rooms that have alternative ventilation systems.

3. Tilt and Turn UPVC Windows

Tilt-and-Turn UPVC window design is extremely low-maintenance. These windows function by tilting inwards or away from the frame to optimise airflow.

They are suitable for controlled ventilation in open, spacious areas of the house. You can also use them in tight spaces where ventilation needs to be controlled.

4. Sliding UPVC Windows

Sliding UPVC window design is a great space savers for modern apartments. They can be fitted to slide horizontally or vertically and may be short or tall. What matters most is that the window frames follow parallel lio that they can slide easily.

Sliding windows have movable panes or have one fixed and one sliding panel. You can even have multiple panels that slide against each other. Another alternative combination of glass and mesh is in the frames.

Why are Aleco's uPVC Windows a better alternative to Steel Windows?

Traditionally, the windows and door market was dominated by steel. There is no doubt that steel is a great material. However, uPVC has emerged as a better and eco-friendly alternative to steel. Here is why uPVC windows /UPVC doors are better than steel ones.

Environment Friendly - uPVC windows/UPVC doors use far less energy to get converted from raw material to end product. On the other end, steel requires enormous energy to turn into an end-product. This difference in energy consumption can run into tonnes.

Apart from this, uPVC windows provide a highly insulated environment as plastic is a bad conductor of heat. On the other hand, steel is a good conductor of heat and winters/summers can be harsh with steel windows.
